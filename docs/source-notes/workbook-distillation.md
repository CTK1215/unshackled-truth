## Week 7: Faith Over Fear (used in the truth card pack)

Key: 2 Timothy 1:7. Memory: "When I am afraid, I put my trust in You."
Psalm 56:3. Spurgeon: "Faith is not the absence of fear, but the presence
of trust in the God, who is greater than our fear."
Teaching: "Fear is one of the greatest obstacles to walking in freedom.
It keeps us clinging to old habits, relationships, or lifestyles because
the unknown feels too risky." · "Faith doesn't mean you'll never feel
fear — it means you choose to trust God anyway."
Lies → Truths: "I can't take that first step — it's too risky." →
Exodus 14:14 · "If I let go of this, I'll have nothing left." →
Isaiah 41:10 · "I'm not strong enough to do this." → 2 Corinthians 12:9.
Faith Builder: "Prison had its dangers, but it was familiar. Stepping
into something new — following God's way — felt like walking blindfolded.
But every step of faith I took, God met me there... every time I chose
faith over fear — I grew stronger."
Actions: read Joshua 1:1-9; pray daily "Lord, help me choose faith over
fear today."; memorize Psalm 56:3.

## Week 8: Grace Over Shame (used in the devotional, days 1-3, 5)

Key: Romans 8:1. Memory: Psalm 103:12. John Murray: "When God forgives,
He does not merely remove the guilt of sin, but He removes the shame of
it as well."
Teaching: "Shame is a heavy weight — it convinces us we are defined by
our worst moments. While guilt says, 'I did something bad.', shame says,
'I am bad.' But God's grace changes that." Grace is: unmerited favor
(Eph 2:8-9), transforming power (Titus 2:11-12), shame-breaking truth.
"Grace calls us out of hiding and into the freedom of being fully known
and fully loved."
Lies → Truths: "I'll always be defined by what I've done." → 2 Cor 5:17 ·
"God forgives me, but I can't forgive myself." → Romans 8:1 · "If people
really knew me, they would reject me." → Romans 8:38-39.
Faith Builder: "I carried shame like a badge — not because I was proud of
what I'd done, but because I believed it was who I was... Grace didn't
just cancel my debt — it rewrote my story."
Exercise: "Rewriting My Story." Challenge: write Psalm 103:12 on a card,
read it every time shame creeps back.

## Week 9: Freedom From Guilt (devotional day 4)

Key: 1 John 1:9. Memory: Psalm 32:1. John Owen: "The blood of Christ is
not only sufficient to pardon the guilt of sin, but also to cleanse the
conscience from its stain."
Teaching: conviction (from God, leads to repentance) vs condemning guilt
(not from Him). "When God forgives, He doesn't keep a record against you.
Neither should you."
Lies → Truths: "I have to keep punishing myself." → Romans 8:1 · "Some
sins are just too big to be forgiven." → Isaiah 1:18 · "God forgave me,
but I can't move on." → Philippians 3:13-14.
Faith Builder: "I used to think feeling guilty was a sign of humility...
holding onto guilt was actually saying, 'Jesus, Your sacrifice wasn't
enough for me.' The day I truly believed that my sins were nailed to the
cross and paid for in full, I felt a freedom I had never experienced. I
didn't have to earn it. I just had to receive it."
Action: "Each time guilt resurfaces, speak Romans 8:1 out loud."
