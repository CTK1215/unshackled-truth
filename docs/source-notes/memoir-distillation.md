# Source notes: The Cracks Beneath the Surface (memoir)

Internal working notes for content creation. Quotes preserve the book's
original punctuation exactly (including its dashes); the no-em-dash rule
applies to published content we write, never to Chris's verbatim quotes'
fidelity when studying them. When quoting the book IN published content,
re-punctuate lightly per the house rule.

Key facts: convicted at 16, took a deal at 18 (36-to-life), served ~27
years across 6 prisons (Tehachapi, Calipatria, Ironwood named), clean
September 2016, paroled 2021, parole ended May 2022. Engaged to Tina,
building an assisted-living facility (RCFE) and Unshackled Truth Media.
Signature motto: "Embrace the discomfort." Recurring imagery: cracks,
masks, chains, light, fire/ashes.

## Family / Stand in the Gap (Dedication, 16, 18, 26, 30, 39)

"You taught me what it means to stand in the gap, to pray without
ceasing, and to love without conditions." (Dedication) · "Your faith
became the quiet force that stood between me and the grave more times
than I can count." (Dedication) · "This story—my story—is also yours."
(Dedication) · Mother, after her lung cancer diagnosis: "I just want to
see you get out of prison. But for that to happen, you need to change."
(26, the turning point) · "My Mom was in the court room every day, and
every time I looked up, her eyes found mine." (18)
Nuance: the relationship was complicated (she left when he was 15, Ch 7;
didn't believe him about David, Ch 12); redemption arc resolves in Ch 39:
"Healing has a way of softening judgment."

## Reentry disciplines (31-36) + Call to Action

Parole prep: chapel clerk at Ironwood; mentor Smitty; "I would write and
rewrite my statements... and read every word to him." Inside: daily
prayer, Bible as "a mirror" (28), memorized anchor verses (2 Cor 5:17,
John 8:32), accountability brothers, walked away from prison politics,
two degrees, mentoring. Outside: kept mentor contact, intentionality,
patience through job/license rejections (RCFE cert denied; Tina got
certified instead), career as "one of the biggest factors in staying
free," left a toxic relationship (34-35), built partnership with Tina
"through consistency, honesty, and a shared vision" (36).
Call to Action, 5 steps: 1 "Pick up the Word. Start with John's Gospel.
Read it slowly." 2 "Pray honestly... talk to Him like you would a
friend." 3 "Reflect deeply. Write down what God shows you." 4 "Don't
walk alone. Find a local church, recovery group, or community."
5 "Live unshackled. Don't wait for perfect conditions."

## Voice characteristics

wasn't... I wasn't..."); rhetorical questions as internal monologue;
capitalized theological nouns (Light, Grace, Truth, Word); recovery
directness plus preacher cadence in the epilogue; chapters end on
one-line gut punches; signs "Chris K."; addresses the reader as "you"
in the closing chapters.
